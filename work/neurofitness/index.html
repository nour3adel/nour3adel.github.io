NeuroFitness App

NeuroFitness is a cutting-edge Workout Helper app that leverages deep learning and AI to optimize your fitness routine. It accurately counts both correct and incorrect reps, provides real-time feedback, and classifies each exercise you perform. Your workouts are saved in your history, allowing you to track your progress over time.

Gym exercises are more complex than they appear, and proper movement is essential for both effectiveness and safety. Correct angles and techniques are crucial to avoid risks and potential injuries. While personal trainers can provide guidance, they can be expensive. This underscores the need for affordable and innovative solutions that can guide and correct movements during workouts. Improper form and technique can be particularly harmful, especially in weightlifting exercises, where injuries are most common.

NeuroFitness app helps to ensure correct exercise form by using your camera or uploading video to track movements and verify proper execution. Receive instant feedback to correct your form in real-time, making workouts safer and more effective. The system adapts to different exercises in any gym environment. Track your progress over time, with the app saving and displaying your performance to keep you motivated.

The NeuroFitness app features login, registration, and password recovery pages with OTP verification. It includes a comprehensive library covering 24 muscle groups, each with a variety of workouts targeting all body muscles. All workouts are stored in a database. Additionally, the app utilizes a classification model to identify and track the workouts you perform.

After facing challenges with MySQL and PHP, and experiencing suboptimal performance with Flask for both backend and frontend, we transitioned to the Django framework. Django proved to be more efficient and better suited for our project requirements.

Initially, we attempted to develop a model to evaluate the correctness of workouts. However, due to limited data and slow performance, the results were unsatisfactory. We then switched to using Mediapipe and mathematical techniques to measure angles and distances between body parts, which yielded better results. For classification, we experimented with various deep learning models and ultimately chose the pretrained Inception V3 model. We fine-tuned this model with customized callbacks and hyperparameters to meet our specific needs.
